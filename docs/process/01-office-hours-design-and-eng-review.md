## Problem Statement

Orbital's beta users are commercial real estate lawyers doing due diligence. The product
gives fast answers over uploaded documents, but users do not trust the answers. Two
partners describe being "confidently wrong" as worse than being slow, and one associate
churned after the tool cited a clause that did not exist. The answers that carry no source
reference are both the most disliked and the most dangerous, and there is no way for a
lawyer to verify a claim short of manually hunting through the PDF themselves.

The task: make every answer verifiable at the point of the claim, and make the absence of
grounding visible instead of hidden, so lawyers can trust what they act on.

## Demand Evidence

Quantitative (from `data/usage_events.csv`, linking each `feedback_given` rating to the
response that preceded it):

- Answers citing **0 sources get an 80% thumbs-down rate** (8 of 10); answers with **>=1
  citation get 18.5%** (17 of 92). A ~4x swing, the cleanest correlation in the dataset.
- **16% of responses (49 of 302) cite nothing.** Negative feedback is concentrated almost
  entirely in this bucket.
- Overall 25% of all ratings are negative.
- Engagement is leaking: **weekly active users 35 -> 30 -> 19** over the 3-week beta;
  12 of 50 users (24%) submitted <=2 prompts then bounced. Consistent with a trust problem,
  though per-user causation is not clean at this n (noted honestly, not overclaimed).

Qualitative (from `data/customer_feedback.md`), overwhelmingly about trust:

- "When it tells me it's from section 4.2 of the lease, it's magic. When it doesn't cite
  anything specific, I have to go find it myself anyway, so what's the point?" (Firm A)
- "I'd pay double the licence fee if the AI would just tell me when it's not sure rather
  than making something up. A confidence indicator would change everything." (Firm A)
- "cited a clause that doesn't exist. She doesn't trust it now... being confidently wrong
  is worse than being slow." (Firm B)
- "twice now it's given me an answer that sounds completely authoritative and is just...
  not in the document. That's terrifying when you're advising on a £40M acquisition." (Firm A)

## Status Quo

Today, a lawyer reads an answer, does not fully trust it, opens the PDF in the reader panel,
and manually Ctrl+F's the relevant clause to confirm it before relying on it. The Firm A
quote names the cost exactly: "I have to go find it myself anyway, so what's the point?"
The real competitor is manual verification, not another AI tool.

The app already *asks* the model to cite in prose ("section 4.2") and counts citations with
a brittle regex (`section \d+`, `clause \d+`, ...) in `count_sources_cited`. So this is not
"add citations from zero." Prose citations are unverifiable and the count is cosmetic.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**User:** a commercial real estate associate/partner reviewing a set of deal documents,
who will not act on a claim they cannot verify against the source.

**Narrowest wedge (shippable in the ~90 min box):** every factual claim in an answer carries
a numbered citation chip backed by a **verbatim quote that the server has confirmed exists in
the cited document**. A Sources footer lists each verified quote and its document; clicking a
chip switches the reader to that document. Answers that produce **zero verified citations**
(when documents exist) render with a visible "Unverified" treatment rather than looking
authoritative. Pixel-perfect scroll-to-highlight inside the PDF is explicitly a stretch, not
part of the wedge.

## Constraints

- Total remaining take-home budget is tight (~90 min for this feature). Focused and polished
  beats exhaustive. Scoping under pressure is part of the assessment.
- Must not regress Part 1 (multi-document conversations, additive uploads, tile switching,
  cross-document answers with attribution).
- Existing stack: FastAPI + PydanticAI (Anthropic, `claude-haiku-4-5`), streaming SSE,
  PostgreSQL/SQLAlchemy, React + Vite + Tailwind + shadcn, react-pdf viewer with a text layer.
- No authentication / multi-tenant model (single-user local assumption) — out of scope.

## Premises

1. The churn driver is "confidently wrong," and in this dataset uncited answers are largely
   the same answers as the hallucinated ones (Firm B's fabrication "cited a clause that
   doesn't exist" — the hallucination already came with a citation). AGREED.
2. The core of the feature is **server-side verification that each quoted span exists
   verbatim (normalized) in the cited document**. The chip is the surface; the verification
   is the product. Unverified citation UI on a legal tool amplifies the failure it means to
   fix. AGREED.
3. A quote that fails verification is not hidden — it becomes the honest "unverified,
   couldn't ground this" signal. That is the confidence indicator users asked for, computed
   from fact rather than the model's self-report. AGREED.
4. Scope guard: within ~90 min, pixel-perfect scroll-to-highlight inside the PDF is the
   gold-plating risk. Minimum lovable = verified chips + click-switches-to-that-document +
   verbatim quote shown; jump-to-page/highlight is a clean stretch. AGREED.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Verified citations, no in-PDF jump (RECOMMENDED — minimal viable)
Model emits inline citation markers carrying `doc_id` + a short verbatim quote. Backend
parses them, verifies each quote exists (whitespace/case-normalized substring) in that
document's `extracted_text`, keeps only verified citations, and replaces the regex
`count_sources_cited` with a real verified count. Frontend renders numbered chips inline
plus a Sources footer (quote + document title); clicking a chip selects that document in the
existing viewer tiles. Answers with zero verified grounding (when docs exist) get an amber
"Unverified" treatment. Effort S/M, Risk Low. Reuses the SSE pipeline, tile switching, and
markdown rendering. No DB migration (citations live inline in the stored message content;
the verified count uses the existing `sources_cited` column).

### Approach B: A + jump-to-source in the PDF (ideal)
Everything in A, plus clicking a citation switches the document and scrolls the react-pdf
text layer to the page holding the quote and highlights it. This is the "section 4.2 =
magic" experience and also satisfies the trainee's "jump to the clause" ask. Effort L,
Risk Med (pdf.js text-layer: locating a quote across span boundaries, normalization, and a
highlight overlay is the time sink). Reuses all of A's backend unchanged.

### Approach C: Grounding meter per answer (lateral)
One trust badge per answer (grounded / partial / ungrounded) from verified-quote coverage,
with an expandable evidence list, instead of per-claim chips. Effort S, Risk Med. Softer and
less actionable than per-claim; drifts back toward the self-reported "confidence indicator"
we deliberately avoided, and loses the "which sentence rests on what" that makes an answer
verifiable.

## Recommended Approach

**Approach A now, built so B is a pure additive follow-on.** It delivers the
verification-first trust mechanism inside the time box, ships the honest "unverified" signal
the data demands, and degrades cleanly if B runs out of time. C's grounding meter can be
layered cheaply on top of A's verified counts as a header summary later, but it is not the
core. Sequence within A: (1) backend marker contract + verification + real count; (2)
frontend chip parser + Sources footer + click-to-select-document; (3) "Unverified"
treatment. Then, only if time holds, add B's jump-to-page + highlight.

## Implementation Sketch

Backend (`services/llm.py`, `routers/messages.py`):
- **doc_id provenance:** the `<document id="..." title="...">` blocks already inject each
  document's real `doc_id` into the prompt (see `_build_document_blocks`). The system prompt
  instructs the model to cite using ONLY those ids. A marker whose `doc_id` is not one of the
  conversation's documents is treated as **unverified** (same path as a quote that fails to
  match), never silently accepted.
- System prompt: instruct the model to attach an inline citation immediately after each
  factual claim using a delimited marker that carries the source `doc_id` and a SHORT
  verbatim quote copied exactly from that document, e.g. `[[cite:<doc_id>::<verbatim quote>]]`.
  Quote must be a contiguous span from the document, <= ~20 words, containing no `]]`. Keep
  the existing NOT-FOUND / conflict / injection-defence rules.
- **Parsing / delimiter rule:** anchor on the literal opening `[[cite:`, take `doc_id` as
  everything up to the FIRST `::` (our ids are 16-char hex, no `::`), and the quote as
  everything after that first `::` up to the closing `]]` (non-greedy). Quotes may legally
  contain `:` and `]` (but not `]]`); splitting on the first `::` only makes that safe.
- New `verify_citations(full_response, documents) -> (clean_text, citations, verified_count)`:
  regex-parse markers, normalize both sides (see normalization below), confirm each quote is a
  substring of the cited document's `extracted_text`. **Never silently drop.** A marker whose
  quote verifies becomes a `verified` citation; a marker that fails (bad doc_id, quote not
  found, or only a near-match below threshold) becomes an `unverified` citation that is still
  shown to the user, flagged, so the model is not rewarded for simply omitting citations.
- **Normalization (hard output of The Assignment spike; minimum set to implement):** collapse
  runs of whitespace to a single space, case-fold, map curly quotes to straight, and strip
  soft hyphens (U+00AD) before substring comparison. If exact-substring fidelity proves poor
  in the spike, fall back to a near-match threshold (e.g. normalized token overlap) that marks
  the citation `unverified` rather than `verified` when below threshold — degrade to honest,
  never to false-confident.
- **`sources_cited` semantics:** set to the count of **verified** citations only. Unverified
  (flagged) citations do not count. This replaces the `count_sources_cited` regex.
- Streaming: markers stream inline in the existing `content` events (they live in the text),
  so no new SSE event type is required for A; the final `message` event already carries the
  full content that the frontend parses. Consider a `citations` field on the final message
  for convenience, but content-embedded markers are the source of truth (survives reload with
  no schema change).
- **Streaming render rule (frontend, but the contract lives here):** the raw quote text inside
  a marker must never render as prose. When the parser hits an unmatched `[[cite:` it buffers
  from there to end-of-stream and renders nothing for that span until the closing `]]` arrives;
  verification runs only on the final complete text, not on partial stream chunks.

Frontend (`components/MessageBubble.tsx`, `DocumentViewer.tsx`, `App.tsx`, `types.ts`):
- Parse `[[cite:...]]` markers out of message content (both streamed and reloaded) into
  numbered chips rendered at the claim, with a Sources footer listing each quote + document
  title. Buffer partial markers during streaming per the render rule above so tokens don't
  flash.
- **Numbering / dedup:** number citations sequentially in text order; dedupe identical
  (`doc_id`, normalized quote) pairs to a single footer entry and reuse its number if the same
  citation appears twice. Verified chips render normally; unverified chips render visually
  distinct (e.g. amber/struck) with a "couldn't verify this in the document" tooltip.
- Clicking a chip calls the existing `selectDocument(doc_id)` (lift the handler into `App.tsx`
  so `MessageBubble` can trigger a viewer switch).
- **Answer-level "Unverified" treatment** fires only for an answer that makes factual claims
  yet has zero verified citations while the conversation has documents. Explicit refusals /
  NOT-FOUND / clarifying-question replies (which correctly cite nothing) are excluded so they
  are not falsely flagged. Render an amber "Unverified — not grounded in your documents;
  verify before relying on it" banner instead of the "N sources cited" line. (Simplest robust
  signal: fire when documents exist and verified count is 0 and the reply is not a
  NOT-FOUND/refusal; detecting the latter can reuse the existing not-found phrasing.)

## Success Criteria

- Every factual claim in a grounded answer carries a chip whose quote is confirmed to exist
  verbatim in the cited document (spot-checked against fixtures, including the malicious-PDF

- Clicking a chip switches the reader panel to the correct document.
- An answer the model cannot ground renders the "Unverified" treatment, never a confident
  uncited answer.
- `sources_cited` reflects verified citations, not regex hits.

## Eng Review — Locked Decisions (2026-07-04)

Reviewed against the actual code. Every sketch item maps to a real file. Decisions below are
folded into the build contract.

### D1 — Stored content carries the verdict (was under-specified)
`verify_citations` returns `clean_text` where the raw model markers are **rewritten to an
adjudicated inline form** (`[[c:1]]`, `[[c:2]]`, ...) plus an appended machine-readable Sources
block encoding `{n, doc_id, quote, verified}` per citation. This is what gets saved to
`Message.content`. No schema change, no migration; the verdict is computed once at generation
and survives reload (frontend re-parses the resolved form, never the raw grammar). `sources_cited`
= count of `verified` citations only (single authoritative number, derived from the same pass).

### D2 — Amber "Unverified" must NOT fire on upload summaries (correctness bug)
`count_sources_cited` is called in TWO places: `messages.py:171` (chat) and `messages.py:293`
(`summarize_uploaded_documents`). Summaries carry no citation markers, so verified_count=0 with
docs present would falsely trigger the amber banner on the friendly orientation message. Fix:
mark summary messages as a non-answer kind and exclude them from the amber treatment (alongside
the existing NOT-FOUND/refusal/clarifying exclusions). Amber fires only on replies to a user
question.

### D3 — One shared parser; raw markers must never reach Streamdown
Assistant text renders through `<Streamdown>` (`MessageBubble.tsx:55,81`) and `consumeStream`
(`use-messages.ts:42`) appends raw chunks. Without intervention, raw `[[cite:...]]` fragments
flash as literal text mid-stream. Rule: **parse first, render markdown only on non-marker
segments.** During streaming, suppress any open `[[cite:` span (buffer across SSE chunk
boundaries) to end-of-marker — verification only runs on the final complete text, so no live
chip is shown, the marker span is just hidden. On the final `message` event and on reload, parse
the D1 resolved form into chips + footer.

### D4 — Inline = plain superscript numbers; interactive chips live in the Sources footer
Inline clickable chips inside Streamdown-rendered markdown is the frontend trap (parallel to the
PDF-jump stretch). Inline citations render as plain markdown superscript numbers; the Sources
footer entries are the clickable surface that calls `selectDocument(doc_id)`. (Cross-model note:
Codex would cut inline entirely to footer-only + answer badge; we keep lightweight inline numbers
because they preserve "which sentence rests on what" at near-zero cost.)

### Folded from Code Quality / Performance / Outside Voice
- **CQ1** — Document the marker grammar once (spec comment atop `verify_citations`) and use the
  SAME literal test strings as fixtures on the Python side and the TS resolved-form side so a
  grammar change breaks both suites together.
- **PERF1** — Normalize each document's `extracted_text` ONCE per request into
  `{doc_id: normalized_text}`, then match all quotes against the cache. Not per-marker.
- **[Codex #3 — P1] Document-title propagation.** `ChatWindow.tsx:167` passes only `message` to
  `MessageBubble`. Prop-drill a `doc_id → {title}` map (or the documents list) AND the
  `selectDocument` handler through `ChatWindow` into `MessageBubble` so the Sources footer can
  show titles and switch the viewer. Missing dependency in the original sketch.
- **[Codex #6 — P1] HTML-entity unescaping in normalization.** `_build_document_blocks` XML-escapes
  the document body (`llm.py:68`), so the model sees `&amp;`/`&lt;` and may copy them into a quote.
  Normalization must `html.unescape` the model quote before substring comparison against the raw
  `extracted_text`. Add to the normalization set: collapse whitespace, case-fold, curly→straight
  quotes, strip U+00AD soft hyphens, **html.unescape**.
- **[Codex #15 — CRITICAL regression] Existing test breaks.** `test_document_and_prompt.py:59`
  asserts the old regex `count_sources_cited(...) == 3`. Replacing/re-semanticizing that function
  breaks this test. Update it to assert the new verified-count semantics — mandatory, no defer.
- **[Codex #14 — demo risk] All-amber fallback.** If the model stops emitting markers, every
  answer goes amber and the feature looks broken. Mitigation: the Assignment spike de-risks
  quote-fidelity first; pick a reliably-cited question for the Loom. No auto-retry (budget).
- **[Codex #16 — positioning] Honest limitation.** A verified quote proves the quote exists, not
  that the prose claim is a fair reading of it. Soften success criteria to "claims the model chose
  to cite are verified; ungrounded answers are flagged" and name this limitation in the Loom. Do
  not market "every factual claim" as guaranteed.
